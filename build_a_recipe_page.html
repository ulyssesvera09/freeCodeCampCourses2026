The ultimate mexican Sopes traditional recipe!!!

When a craving hits, look no further than sopes—a quintessential Mexican street food. As a beloved member of the antojitos family, these satisfying corn cakes feature a crispy fried base piled high with flavorful toppings. While often featuring succulent meats, they are just as delicious loaded with grilled vegetables, beans, and fresh salsa for a vegetarian-friendly option.

Ingredients

    For the base (Masa)

  • 2 cups of corn flour
  • 1 cup of warm water
  • 1 pinch of salt

    • Frying & Assembly

  • Vegetable oil
  • Refried beans

Instructions

  1. Make the dough: Mix masa harina, salt, and warm water to form a soft, pliable dough. Cover and rest for 15 minutes.
  2. Shape the sopes: Roll dough into golf-ball-sized portions. Press into thick, 3-inch rounds using a tortilla press or your hands.
  3. Pre-cook: Cook the rounds on a hot, dry griddle or comal for 1-2 minutes per side until just set but not browned.
  4. Form the ridges: While still hot and pliable, pinch the edges to form a shallow rim (like a mini tart shell).
  5. Fry: In a skillet, heat about ¼ inch of oil. Fry each sope base until golden and crisp, about 1-2 minutes per side. Drain on paper towels.
  6. Assemble: Spread a layer of refried beans inside the warm base. Top with your choice of meat, cheese, lettuce, crema, and salsa. Serve immediately.
